About

Who am I?

I am Rodrigo, a software engineer specializing in transaction processing and distributed systems. I have been working for MemSQL, specifically in the replication and clustering components, for the last three years, and learning and enjoying it a lot.

My main interests align nicely with my work - I enjoy spending time struggling with code, and playing with cutting edge computer technology. This actually weirds out my girlfriend, who often says You don't know how to relax. You can often find me sitting at my desk, staring at a dark terminal, and typing away, with the occasional loud sound of frustration. I also enjoy spending time with people at coffee shops, playing video games, and reading. You'll often find me organizing game nights at my place, where I gather many friends to play random games, and are mostly just a way to hang out and keep in touch.

My goals at the moment are mostly career focused - I still have a lot to learn, and a lot of evolving to do. I'm looking forward to fully running some major projects at MemSQL, and further improving my skills in designing, and building valuable and technically interesting systems. Other goals are to have a corgi in my household in the not-too-far-away future.

What is this Website?

My main goal with this website is to have a place where I can publicly share write-ups of interesting projects I work on. It is also a way for me to explore technologies and areas I don't interact with often (for example, design and user interfaces). Expect to see write-ups about this website, and the infrastructure that serves it.

One of the main self-imposed constraints of this website is that I am building it all from scratch. Could I have made my life easier if I used frameworks or templating engines? Certainly. Could I learn more useful skills if I used the tools professionals use to build web applications/websites (e.g. react, redux, ...)? Certainly. My response as to why I didn't use any of those? I do what I want. I do not intend to switch career tracks, I am fairly happy building systems software (the kind you would use to build a web application on top of), and instead I am more interested in learning the fundamentals, and the struggles that go into building a website/web application. In my personal experience, I have been most successful at doing that when I'm working in a project where I control the vast majority of the code. Also in the spirit of I do what I want, there are no guarantees I will completely follow this constraint.

One other constraint which I intend to follow is that this website should work even if javascript is turned off. I do not mind javascript in websites, but I agree with the sentiment that most websites have gotten extremely bloated, and that the invasive ads and third-party tracking can be a huge issue, especially for the security and privacy conscious of us. This website may look a little different with javascript turned off, and may also lack interactive features that are not possible (or that I have not found out how) to implement without javascript. All functionality should be available, in particular access to all the content. If something does not work, feel free to e-mail me at rodrigo@codeblurb.com
